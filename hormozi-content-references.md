## Carousel & Story Style

### How Hormozi structures carousels:
1. Hook slide: Bold statement, contrarian take, or shocking number
2. Problem slide: "Most people do X wrong because..."
3. Framework slide: Visual equation or numbered steps
4. Example slide: Real numbers, real scenario
5. Proof slide: Results, testimonials, data
6. CTA slide: "Follow for more" or "DM me X"

### His carousel topics:
- Value Equation breakdowns
- Before/after business transformations
- "Stop doing X, start doing Y" format
- Myth-busting common business beliefs
- Step-by-step frameworks with numbers
- "I lost $X million because..." stories

### Story style:
- Raw, unfiltered, face to camera
- Uses whiteboard or pen/paper
- Draws frameworks live
- Short (15-30s per story)
- Always ends with a hook to next story
- Uses polls and questions for engagement

## How to use these references in coaching:

When analyzing a business, reference specific Hormozi teachings:
- For offer problems → reference $100M Offers Value Equation video
- For lead generation → reference $100M Leads and Rule of 100
- For pricing → reference "How to Price Your Product"
- For scaling → reference "4 Ways to Grow Any Business"
- For mindset blocks → reference "The Mindset That Made Me $100M"

Always say: "As Hormozi explains in [specific video/book]..." to ground your advice in his actual teachings.

## Key Hormozi Content Principles:

1. Give away the secrets, sell the implementation
2. Volume > quality (at first) — Rule of 100
3. Every piece of content should make the viewer say "I need to save this"
4. Lead with value, never pitch in content
5. Use specific numbers — "$100M" not "a lot of money"
6. Teach frameworks, not tips
7. Make the viewer feel smarter after consuming your content
8. Controversial > bland — take a stance
9. Personal stories > generic advice
10. Always tie back to a concrete action the viewer can take
