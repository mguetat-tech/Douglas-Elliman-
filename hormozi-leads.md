# $100M Leads — Complete Lead Generation Framework

## The Fundamental Truth

No leads = no business. It doesn't matter how good your offer is if nobody sees it. Lead generation is the lifeblood of every business.

```
Leads  x  Conversion Rate  x  Price  =  Revenue
```

If any of those three numbers is zero, revenue is zero. Most people try to fix conversion or price first. Fix VOLUME first. Volume negates luck.

---

## The 4 Core Ways to Get Leads

Every lead generation method in existence falls into one of these 4 buckets:

|  | **YOU do the work** | **OTHERS do the work** |
|---|---|---|
| **People who know you** | 1. Warm Outreach | 3. Referrals / Affiliates |
| **People who don't know you** | 2. Cold Outreach | 4. Content + Paid Ads |

### 1. Warm Outreach (free, fast, limited)
- Reach out to people who already know you exist
- Friends, family, past colleagues, LinkedIn connections, Instagram followers
- "Hey [Name], I'm building something that helps [avatar] do [result]. Know anyone who might be interested?"
- Your existing followers and leads ARE your warm audience
- This is the fastest path to your next client. Do it TODAY.
- **Volume target:** Contact 100 warm leads this week

### 2. Cold Outreach (free, slower, scalable)
- Reach out to strangers who match your avatar
- Email, LinkedIn DMs, Instagram DMs, cold calls
- Requires a compelling message and a clear offer
- Lower conversion rate but unlimited volume
- **Volume target:** 100 cold outreaches per day

### 3. Referrals / Affiliates (free, high trust, limited control)
- Get your existing customers to send you new customers
- Get partners to send you leads
- Offer incentives: commission, reciprocal referrals, public credit
- Referral leads close at 3-5x the rate of cold leads
- **Action:** Ask every satisfied client for 3 introductions

### 4. Content + Paid Ads (scalable, compound, requires patience)
- Content = free advertising that compounds (reels, posts, articles)
- Paid ads = paid traffic you control (Meta, Google, LinkedIn)
- Content is slow but compounds. Ads are fast but stop when you stop paying.
- **Volume target:** 1 reel per day, or 100 minutes of content creation per day

---

## The Rule of 100

This is non-negotiable. Every single day, do ONE of these:

- **100 outreaches** (DMs, emails, calls) — OR
- **100 minutes of content creation** (reels, posts, carousels)

Do this every day for 100 days. If your business isn't transformed, something else is broken (your offer, your targeting, your pitch).

Most people fail because they do 10 outreaches, get no response, and quit. Volume negates luck. 100/day means you'll hit gold eventually.

## Lead Magnets — The 7-Step Framework

A lead magnet is a free thing you give away that's SO good people would pay for it. It accomplishes 3 things:
1. Attracts the right people (qualifies them)
2. Demonstrates your expertise (builds trust)
3. Creates reciprocity (they owe you attention)

### The 7 Steps to a Killer Lead Magnet:

**Step 1: Pick one specific problem your avatar has**
- Not "grow your business" — too vague
- "How to post on LinkedIn every day without writing a single word" — specific

**Step 2: Solve it completely**
- Don't tease. Don't hold back. Give them the full solution.
- "But then why would they buy?" Because they want YOU to do it, not the instructions.
- Give away the secrets, sell the implementation.

**Step 3: Make it consumable in under 10 minutes**
- PDF checklist, short video, template, swipe file
- Nobody reads a 50-page ebook. Everybody reads a 1-page checklist.

**Step 4: Name it for the outcome, not the format**
- Bad: "Free PDF Guide"
- Good: "The 7 Automations That Replace a Full-Time Employee"

**Step 5: Make it look premium**
- Design matters. A beautiful PDF converts better than an ugly one.
- It's the first impression of your brand.

**Step 6: Deliver it instantly**
- No "check your email in 24 hours." Instant delivery = instant gratification.
- DM delivery or instant download is ideal.

**Step 7: Include a clear next step**
- At the end of the lead magnet: "Want me to set this up for you? Book a call."
- The lead magnet warms them up. The CTA moves them forward.

**Question to ask:** Is each lead magnet solving a BURNING problem for your specific avatar? Or are they generic?

## Warm Audience Strategies

Your warm audience (followers, leads, past customers) is the easiest money you'll ever make.

### The Warm Outreach Script:
```
Hey [Name],

I noticed [something specific about them or their business].

I'm working with [type of business] to [specific result].

Would you be open to a quick chat to see if it could help [their business]?

No pressure either way.
```

### Reactivation Campaign:
- You have leads sitting in your database
- Most of them will never buy unless you reach out again
- Send a value-first message every 2 weeks
- Every 4th message can include a soft CTA
- "Hey, just built something cool — [link to free resource]. Thought of you."

### Customer Referral Script:
```
Hey [Customer Name],

Really glad [specific result] is working for you.

Quick question — do you know 2-3 other business owners who might benefit
from the same thing? Happy to give them a free audit.

No worries if not. Just thought I'd ask.
```

## Cold Outreach That Works

Cold outreach gets a bad reputation because most people do it terribly. Here's how to do it right:

### The Framework: Lead with Value
1. **Who are you?** (1 sentence — credibility)
2. **Why them specifically?** (1 sentence — personalization)
3. **What's in it for them?** (1-2 sentences — value proposition)
4. **Low-friction CTA** (1 sentence — not "buy my thing" but "want me to show you?")

### The Sequence (Day 0 / Day 3 / Day 7):
- **Day 0:** Initial outreach — value-first, no pitch
- **Day 3:** Follow-up with proof (case study, screenshot, testimonial)
- **Day 7:** Break-up message — "No worries if the timing isn't right. Just didn't want you to miss out."

### Rules:
- Personalize the first line. Always.
- Never assume they have a problem. Frame it as an OPPORTUNITY.
- Make it about them, not about you.
- 1-3% response rate is normal for cold email. That means you need volume.
- 100 emails/day x 2% response x 30% close = 18 clients/month at scale

## Content Strategy: Build Once, Sell Forever

Content is the ultimate leverage play. Every reel, every post, every carousel is a salesperson that works 24/7.

### The Content Machine:
1. **1 reel per day** — 60 seconds, one idea, one CTA
2. **1 carousel per week** — deep-dive on a specific problem
3. **1 LinkedIn post per day** — thought leadership for the B2B audience
4. **Keyword trigger on every post** — capture the intent (e.g., "Comment AUDIT")

### Content principles:
- **Hook in 1 second** — pattern interrupt, bold claim, or question
- **Teach the WHAT and WHY, sell the HOW** — "Here's what to automate and why it matters. Want me to set it up? Comment AUDIT."
- **Every post earns or teaches** — if it does neither, don't post it
- **Consistency > virality** — 1 reel/day for 365 days beats 1 viral reel
- **Repurpose everything** — 1 reel becomes 1 carousel, 1 LinkedIn post, 1 email, 1 story

### Scaling content:
- Phase 1 (early): You create everything yourself — 1/day
- Phase 2 ($10K/month): Hire an editor — you film, they edit — 2/day
- Phase 3 ($30K/month): Content team — you ideate, they execute — 3/day across platforms

## The Lead Generation Math

Do YOUR math with these formulas:

```
Goal: [YOUR MONTHLY REVENUE TARGET]

Price per client: [YOUR PRICE]
Clients needed per month: [REVENUE TARGET / PRICE]

Close rate (estimate): 30%
Calls needed per month: [CLIENTS NEEDED / CLOSE RATE]

Call booking rate (estimate): 25%
Leads needed per month: [CALLS NEEDED / BOOKING RATE]

Leads needed per day: [LEADS PER MONTH / 30]
```

Now the question is: which of the 4 channels can consistently deliver that many qualified leads per day?

- **Warm outreach:** Yes, if you work your database hard
- **Cold outreach:** Yes, at 100/day volume
- **Content:** Yes, if posting daily with a growing audience
- **Paid ads:** Yes, but needs budget and testing

**The answer is: do at least 2 of these simultaneously.** Content + warm outreach is the best combo when starting. Add cold outreach when content is systematized.
