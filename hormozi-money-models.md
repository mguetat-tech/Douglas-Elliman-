# $100M Money Models — Business Growth Frameworks

## The Only 4 Ways to Grow a Business

Every business in history grows through exactly 4 levers:

```
Revenue = Customers  x  ARPU  x  Frequency  x  Retention
```

1. **Get more customers** — lead generation, marketing, sales
2. **Increase average revenue per user (ARPU)** — raise prices, upsell, cross-sell
3. **Increase purchase frequency** — get them to buy more often
4. **Increase retention** — keep them longer, reduce churn

Most entrepreneurs obsess over #1 and ignore #2, #3, and #4. That's backwards. Getting a new customer costs 5-25x more than keeping one.

---

## The Profit Equation

```
Profit = Revenue - Cost of Goods Sold - Operating Expenses
```

There are only 2 ways to increase profit:
1. **Make more money** (revenue up)
2. **Spend less money** (costs down)

If your margin is already high, the entire game is revenue growth. Don't optimize costs that are already low. Optimize sales.

---

## LTV vs CAC — The Only Ratio That Matters

**LTV** = Lifetime Value of a Customer
**CAC** = Cost to Acquire a Customer

```
LTV / CAC > 3  →  Healthy business
LTV / CAC > 5  →  Printing money
LTV / CAC < 2  →  You're bleeding
```

### How to calculate LTV:
```
LTV = Average Revenue per Customer  x  Average Lifespan (in months)  x  Gross Margin
```

Example: If a client pays $3,000 once and your margin is 90%:
- LTV = $3,000 x 1 x 0.90 = $2,700

If you add a $300/month retainer and they stay 6 months:
- LTV = $3,000 + ($300 x 6) x 0.90 = $4,320

### How to increase LTV:
- Raise prices (duh)
- Add recurring revenue (monthly retainer, support, updates)
- Add back-end offers (upsell after they succeed with offer 1)
- Increase retention through better results and relationships

## Front-End vs Back-End Offers

### Front-End Offer
- Purpose: acquire the customer, even at breakeven or small profit
- Low barrier to entry
- Proves you can deliver
- Builds trust
- Example: $500 audit or free lead magnet

### Back-End Offer
- Purpose: where you make the real money
- Sold to existing customers who already trust you
- Higher price, higher margin
- Example: $3,000 program, $10,000 done-for-you, $500/month retainer

**The money is in the back end.** The front end is just the door.

## The Ascension Model

Structure your offers like a ladder. Each step is higher price, higher value, deeper relationship.

```
Level 0: FREE — Lead magnet, free content, audit
         Purpose: Get them into your world

Level 1: LOW ($100-500) — Template, mini-course, quick win
         Purpose: Turn a stranger into a customer

Level 2: MID ($1,000-5,000) — Formation, coaching, done-with-you
         Purpose: Deliver the core transformation

Level 3: HIGH ($5,000-25,000) — Done-for-you, VIP, implementation
         Purpose: Maximum result, maximum revenue

Level 4: RECURRING ($200-1,000/month) — Retainer, community, support
         Purpose: Ongoing value = ongoing revenue
```

You don't need all levels on day 1. Start with Level 2 (your core offer). Add Level 4 (retainer) next. Then add Level 3 (high-ticket for premium clients).

## High Ticket vs Low Ticket — The Math

### Low Ticket ($500)
- Need 200 customers to make $100K
- High volume, high churn, lots of support
- Needs ads, funnels, automation at scale
- Low touch, low relationship

### High Ticket ($5,000)
- Need 20 customers to make $100K
- Low volume, high quality, deep relationship
- Needs calls, proof, trust
- High touch, high retention

**At early stages, high ticket is the only move.** You don't have the audience or the systems for low ticket yet. Sell fewer things at higher prices to better clients.

## Continuity: The Recurring Revenue Play

One-time revenue is a treadmill. You start every month at zero. Recurring revenue is a snowball.

**Models for recurring revenue:**
1. **Monthly retainer** — ongoing support, updates, maintenance ($300-1,000/month)
2. **Subscription community** — access to group, resources, templates ($50-200/month)
3. **Performance-based** — you get paid a % of results you generate
4. **Licensing/SaaS** — they pay monthly to use your tools

**Your move:** After your core offer, add a monthly retainer where you maintain their systems and answer questions. 10 clients on retainer at $300-500/month = $3,000-5,000/month of baseline revenue before you sell anything new.

## The 4 Types of Leverage

From Naval Ravikant, endorsed by Hormozi:

### 1. Labor (people)
- Other people working for you
- Linear scale: 1 person = 1x output
- Expensive, requires management
- Your eventual move: hire for delivery

### 2. Capital (money)
- Other people's money working for you
- Nonlinear scale: capital compounds
- Not relevant at early stages

### 3. Code (technology)
- Software working for you 24/7
- Infinite scale: build once, sell forever
- Automations, tools, and systems are code leverage

### 4. Media (content)
- Content working for you 24/7
- Infinite scale: one reel reaches 100K people
- Every piece of content is a permanent sales rep
- Compounds over time

**The playbook:** Stack Code + Media first. Then add Labor when revenue justifies it. Capital comes last.

## Revenue Stages and Their Constraints

### $0 to $100K — THE OFFER STAGE
- **Constraint:** Your offer isn't good enough or clear enough
- **Fix:** Get the offer right. One offer, one avatar, one channel
- **Trap:** Building products instead of selling. Tweaking instead of shipping.
- **Rule:** Sell before you build. If no one buys the idea, building it is a waste.

### $100K to $1M — THE SALES STAGE
- **Constraint:** You can't sell consistently or you're the bottleneck
- **Fix:** Build a repeatable sales process. Hire your first salesperson or setter.
- **Trap:** Trying to add more offers instead of selling the current one better.
- **Rule:** Do the same thing that got you to $100K, but do it 10x more.

### $1M to $10M — THE SYSTEMS STAGE
- **Constraint:** Everything runs through you. You're the bottleneck for delivery.
- **Fix:** Build systems, SOPs, and hire for delivery. Remove yourself from fulfillment.
- **Trap:** Hiring too fast, losing quality, burning cash.
- **Rule:** Document everything. If it's in your head, it doesn't scale.

### $10M to $100M — THE TEAM STAGE
- **Constraint:** Leadership and culture. The business outgrows your management skills.
- **Fix:** Hire leaders, not doers. Build a management layer.
- **Trap:** Micromanaging, not letting go, hiring friends.
- **Rule:** Your job is to build the team that builds the business.
