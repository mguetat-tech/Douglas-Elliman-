# $100M Offers — Complete Framework

## The Core Idea

Most businesses don't have a traffic problem or a conversion problem. They have an OFFER problem. If your offer is good enough, you barely need marketing. If your offer sucks, no amount of marketing will save it.

A **Grand Slam Offer** is an offer so good that people feel stupid saying no.

---

## The Value Equation

This is the most important equation in business:

```
                Dream Outcome  x  Perceived Likelihood of Achievement
Value = ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
                Time Delay  x  Effort & Sacrifice
```

### The 4 Levers

**1. Dream Outcome (increase it)**
- What does the customer REALLY want? Not your product — the end state.
- "I don't want a tool. I want the result the tool produces."
- Paint the after picture so vividly they can taste it.
- The bigger and more specific the dream outcome, the more they'll pay.

**2. Perceived Likelihood of Achievement (increase it)**
- They need to believe it will work FOR THEM, not just in theory.
- Proof: testimonials, case studies, screenshots, live demos, guarantees.
- Specificity increases believability. "Save time" = weak. "Save 12 hours/week on content creation" = strong.
- Social proof from people LIKE them (same industry, same size, same struggles).

**3. Time Delay (decrease it)**
- How fast do they get the first result? The first WIN?
- First 24 hours: send them a quick win immediately after purchase.
- First week: visible progress they can show someone.
- First month: measurable business impact.
- The faster the result, the more valuable the offer.

**4. Effort & Sacrifice (decrease it)**
- What do they have to DO to get the result?
- Done-for-you > done-with-you > do-it-yourself (in terms of perceived value).
- Remove steps. Remove decisions. Remove friction.
- "You show up to the call, we handle everything else."

## Building the Grand Slam Offer — Step by Step

### Step 1: Identify the Starving Crowd

Before you build the offer, find the RIGHT market.

**The 3 criteria for a starving crowd:**
1. **Massive pain** — they're desperate to solve this problem
2. **Purchasing power** — they can actually pay
3. **Easy to target** — you can find and reach them
4. **Growing market** — the trend is in your favor

Don't sell ice to Eskimos. Sell water in the desert.

### Step 2: List Every Problem

Write down every single problem, fear, and concern your customer has related to your domain.

For each problem, think about:
- What happens BEFORE they buy (objections)
- What happens DURING the process (obstacles)
- What happens AFTER they get the result (sustainability)

### Step 3: Turn Problems into Solutions

For every problem, create a solution. Every solution becomes a component of your offer.

The 4 types of solutions:
1. **1-on-1** — highest value, lowest scale (coaching calls, done-for-you)
2. **Small group** — high value, moderate scale (cohorts, group coaching)
3. **One-to-many** — moderate value, high scale (courses, templates)
4. **Self-serve** — lowest value, highest scale (tools, automations)

### Step 4: Build the Offer Stack

Your core offer is the main thing. Then you stack value on top:

**Core Offer:** The main deliverable

**Bonus 1:** Something that solves the NEXT problem (e.g., template library)
**Bonus 2:** Something that reduces effort (e.g., pre-built assets)
**Bonus 3:** Something that reduces time delay (e.g., quick-start guide)
**Bonus 4:** Something that increases certainty (e.g., community access)

Each bonus should be:
- Easy for you to deliver (low cost)
- High perceived value for them
- Addressing a specific objection or fear

### Step 5: Add the Guarantee

The guarantee removes the last barrier: risk.

**Types of guarantees:**
- **Unconditional** — "If you don't love it, full refund, no questions asked"
- **Conditional** — "If you do X, Y, Z and don't get result, full refund"
- **Anti-guarantee** — "All sales final" (works for ultra-premium)
- **Performance-based** — "If you don't get [specific result], I'll work with you for free until you do"
- **Outcome-based** — "You'll get [specific result] or I'll refund you AND pay you $500"

The bolder the guarantee, the more confidence you project. And paradoxically, refund rates go DOWN with stronger guarantees.

### Step 6: Create Urgency and Scarcity

These must be REAL, not manufactured.

**Real scarcity examples:**
- "I can only take 5 clients per month because I do the calls myself"
- "This cohort starts May 1st, next one isn't until September"
- "I'm raising prices to $5,000 on June 1st"

**Real urgency examples:**
- "Every week you wait, that's another $2,000 in lost opportunities"
- "Your competitors are already doing this"

### Step 7: Name the Offer

The name should communicate the result, not the process.

**Bad:** "8-Session Training Package"
**Good:** "The 90-Day Revenue Machine"

**Bad:** "Monthly Consulting Retainer"
**Good:** "The Growth Accelerator"

The name should pass the "cocktail party test" — if they told a friend about it, would the friend immediately understand the value?

## Pricing the Offer

### The rules:
1. **Price on value, not cost.** Your cost is irrelevant. What's it WORTH to the customer?
2. **Price creates perception.** $500 = "maybe this works." $5,000 = "this better work." $5,000 also means the client takes it seriously and actually does the work.
3. **Higher price = better clients.** People who pay more, pay attention. People who pay less, complain more.
4. **The goal is to be so good they'd pay 10x.** Then charge 3x and they're thrilled.

### How to present the price:
- Stack the value FIRST. Show them the total value of everything they're getting.
- Then reveal the price as a fraction of the value.
- "The total value of everything you're getting is over $25,000. Your investment is $3,000. That's 12 cents on the dollar."
- Compare to the cost of NOT solving the problem. "You're losing $2,000/month. In 2 months, this pays for itself."

## Common Offer Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Selling the process, not the outcome
- Bad: "8 sessions of training"
- Good: "In 8 weeks, your business runs on autopilot while you focus on growth"
- Nobody buys a drill. They buy a hole in the wall.

### Mistake 2: Too many options
- 3+ choices = decision paralysis = no decision
- Offer ONE thing. If they want more, upsell later.
- "But what about different budgets?" — If they can't afford your offer, they're not your customer.

### Mistake 3: Weak or missing guarantee
- No guarantee = all risk on the buyer = lower conversion
- "But what if people abuse the guarantee?" — Refund rates with guarantees are typically 5-10%. The extra conversions more than cover it.

### Mistake 4: Underpricing
- Charging $500 for a $5,000 result = you look amateur
- Low prices attract bad clients who demand the most
- Price is a signal. What is your price signaling?

### Mistake 5: No urgency
- If they can buy "anytime," they buy "never"
- Create real constraints: limited spots, cohort start dates, price increases
- The urgency must be TRUE, never manufactured

## The Offer Creation Checklist

When building or reviewing an offer, walk through every item:

**Market:**
- [ ] Is this a starving crowd with purchasing power?
- [ ] Can I find and reach them easily?
- [ ] Is the market growing or shrinking?

**Dream Outcome:**
- [ ] Can I state the dream outcome in one sentence?
- [ ] Is it specific and measurable? (not "grow your business" but "add $5K/month in automated revenue")
- [ ] Does it resonate emotionally AND logically?

**Proof:**
- [ ] Do I have at least 3 testimonials or case studies?
- [ ] Can I show before/after results?
- [ ] Do I have proof from people similar to the prospect?

**Time to Value:**
- [ ] First win within 24-48 hours of purchase?
- [ ] Visible progress within the first week?
- [ ] Full result within 60-90 days?

**Effort Required:**
- [ ] Is the effort required clearly communicated?
- [ ] Have I removed every unnecessary step?
- [ ] Could I make it even easier without sacrificing results?

**Guarantee:**
- [ ] Is there a clear, bold guarantee?
- [ ] Does it remove the buyer's biggest fear?
- [ ] Am I confident enough in my delivery to back it?

**Price:**
- [ ] Is the price anchored to value, not cost?
- [ ] Is it presented after the full value stack?
- [ ] Does it pass the "10x ROI" test?

**Presentation:**
- [ ] Does the offer have a compelling name?
- [ ] Is the stack easy to understand in 60 seconds?
- [ ] Would the prospect feel stupid saying no?

If you can check every box, ship it. If not, fix the gaps first.
