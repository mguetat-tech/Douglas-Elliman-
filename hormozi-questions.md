# Coaching Questions — The Hormozi Interrogation Framework

## How to Use These Questions

When the user brings a problem, decision, or idea — don't jump to answers. Ask questions first. The right question reveals the real problem. A coach who gives answers without asking questions is just guessing.

Pick 2-3 questions from the relevant category. Go deep on the answers. Then give the tactical advice.

---

## Offer Creation

1. What is the dream outcome your customer wakes up wanting?
2. If your client got the perfect result, what would their life look like in 90 days?
3. What are ALL the things that could go wrong between them buying and getting the result?
4. For each obstacle — what could you do to make that obstacle disappear?
5. What would they happily pay $10,000 to avoid?
6. What is the single most valuable thing you could give them that costs you almost nothing?
7. If you could only deliver ONE result, which one would make them say "this was worth 10x what I paid"?
8. What does your customer secretly want but would never say out loud?
9. What are they currently doing to solve this problem? How's that working out for them?
10. If you had to guarantee a result or give the money back, what result would you guarantee?
11. What's the fastest you could deliver the first win? First 24 hours? First week?
12. What would make this offer so good that they'd feel stupid saying no?
13. What would you need to add to make this a no-brainer at twice the price?

## Pricing

14. What is the next best alternative your customer has?
15. How much does it cost them to NOT solve this problem — per month, per year?
16. If this saves them 10 hours a week, what's that worth at their hourly rate?
17. What's the ROI if this works? 2x? 5x? 10x their investment?
18. Are you pricing based on what it costs you or what it's worth to them?
19. If you 3x'd your price tomorrow, what would you need to add to justify it?
20. What would happen if you charged 10x more and served 1/10th the people?
21. Who is already paying 5-10x your price for a similar outcome?
22. What's the most someone has ever paid you? Why did they pay that?
23. If you were the customer and you saw this price, would you think "cheap" or "premium"?
24. What's the lifetime value of a customer if they stay and buy more?
25. Are you competing on price or on value? Pick one.

## Lead Generation

26. Where are your ideal customers already congregating — online and offline?
27. What free thing could you give away that's so good people would pay for it?
28. If you had to get 10 customers this week with zero budget, what would you do?
29. What content could you create that would make your ideal customer think "this person gets me"?
30. How many people did you reach out to today? This week? This month?
31. What's your Rule of 100? Are you doing 100 minutes of content OR 100 outreaches per day?
32. Which of the 4 lead channels are you actually doing? Warm outreach, cold outreach, content, or paid ads?
33. How many of those are you doing consistently for more than 90 days?
34. What would happen if you did ONLY lead generation for the next 30 days?
35. Who already has your ideal customers and would benefit from sending them to you?
36. What's your lead magnet? Does it solve a real problem or is it a glorified brochure?
37. If a stranger saw your content, would they immediately understand what you do and who it's for?

## Sales and Closing

38. What's the perceived likelihood of success in your customer's mind?
39. How can you reduce the time between purchase and first result?
40. How can you reduce the effort required from the customer?
41. What proof do you have that this works? Testimonials, case studies, screenshots, data?
42. What's the biggest objection you hear? How are you currently handling it?
43. What's your close rate? If it's below 30%, your offer is broken.
44. Are you selling to people who need what you have or convincing people they need it?
45. What would happen if you only got on calls with people who were already 80% sold?
46. What guarantee could you offer that would eliminate all risk for the buyer?
47. Do prospects feel MORE certain or LESS certain after talking to you?
48. How long is your sales cycle? What would it take to cut it in half?
49. Are you making ONE clear ask or confusing them with options?
50. When was the last time you followed up with a lead who didn't buy? How many times?

## Scaling

51. What is the #1 constraint in your business right now? Not the top 5. The top 1.
52. If you had to 10x your revenue without working more hours, what would need to change?
53. What are you doing that someone else could do 80% as well?
54. What would you STOP doing if you could only work 2 hours a day?
55. What's the one thing that, if you fixed it, would make everything else easier?
56. Are you building an asset or are you trading time for money with extra steps?
57. What part of your delivery could be automated, templated, or systematized?
58. If you hired one person tomorrow, what would they do all day?
59. What would break first if you got 50 clients next month?
60. Is your current model something you'd want to do with 100 clients? 500?
61. What's the bottleneck: leads, conversion, delivery, or retention?

## Mindset and Decision-Making

62. What would you do if you HAD to make this work or you'd lose everything?
63. What are you avoiding that you know you need to do?
64. If your best friend came to you with this exact situation, what would you tell them?
65. What's the worst case scenario if this fails? Can you survive it?
66. Are you making this decision based on fear or based on data?
67. What would the version of you making $1M/year do differently TODAY?
68. Is this a real problem or a comfortable problem to have so you don't face the real one?
69. What's the decision you've been putting off? Why?
70. Are you optimizing or procrastinating? What's the difference right now?
71. If you could only do ONE thing for the next 90 days, what would move the needle the most?
72. What did you learn from your last failure that you're not applying yet?

## Customer and Market

73. When was the last time you actually talked to a customer? Not sold to — talked to?
74. What do your best customers have in common?
75. Why did your last 3 customers buy? What was the trigger?
76. Why did your last 3 prospects NOT buy? What was the real reason?
77. If you could only serve one type of customer forever, who would it be?
78. What does your customer complain about that your competitors ignore?
79. What promise is your competitor making that they can't deliver on?
80. Are you in a growing market or a shrinking one? How do you know?

## Accountability and Execution

81. What specifically are you going to do on Monday at 9am?
82. How will you know this worked? What's the metric?
83. When will you have this done? Give me a date.
84. What's stopping you from doing this today?
85. Did you do what you said you'd do last time? If not, why?
86. Are we talking about the same problem we talked about last time? Why hasn't it moved?
87. How many hours this week did you spend on revenue-generating activities vs everything else?
88. What did you ship this week? Not plan. Ship.
89. If I checked in with you in 7 days, what will be different?
90. Are you moving fast enough? What would "fast" actually look like?

## Delivery and Operations

91. What's the most common complaint from your customers? What are you doing about it?
92. If you got 50 new clients tomorrow, what would break first?
93. What part of your delivery is still in your head instead of in a system?
94. How long does it take from "they pay" to "they get the first result"?
95. What would you need to document so someone else could deliver at 80% of your quality?
96. Are you delivering what they NEED or what they ASKED FOR? Those are different things.
97. What's the one thing every client thanks you for that you didn't expect?
98. Could you serve 10x more clients without 10x more work? If not, why?

## Partnerships and Team

99. If you could hire one person tomorrow who would free up 80% of your time, what would they do?
100. What task do you hate doing that someone else would love?
101. Who already has access to your ideal customers and would benefit from partnering with you?
102. What's the simplest way to test if a partnership works before going all-in?
103. Are your partners incentivized to win when you win? If not, fix that first.
104. What would change if you had a sales rep doing 20 calls/week for you?

## Offer Refinement

105. If your client could ONLY get one result from working with you, which result would justify the full price?
106. What's the one thing your competitors promise but can't actually deliver?
107. If you had to make your offer work for clients who are twice as busy, what would you change?
108. What's the version of your offer that costs you zero extra time but doubles the perceived value?
109. If you raised your price by 50% tomorrow, what would you need to add or change to justify it?
110. What testimonial don't you have yet that would make your offer undeniable?

## Content and Lead Generation Deep-Dive

111. What piece of content brought you the most clients? Why did it work?
112. If you could only post on one platform for the next year, which one and why?
113. What question does your avatar Google at 2am when they can't sleep?
114. What free thing could you give away that would make them think "if the free stuff is this good..."?
115. What's the one post you're afraid to publish? That's probably the one you should publish.
116. Are you creating content for your audience or for yourself?

---

## Meta-Questions (When You're Stuck on What to Ask)

117. What's the real question behind the question you're asking me?
118. What would change if you already knew the answer?
119. What are you hoping I'll say?
120. What would you do if no one was watching?
121. What's the scariest version of the truth right now?
122. Are you asking for advice or asking for permission?
123. What do you already know you should do that you're avoiding?

---

## How to Use These in a Coaching Session

When the user brings a topic, follow this sequence:

1. **Diagnose first** — Ask 2-3 questions from the relevant category to understand the real situation
2. **Reflect back** — "So what I'm hearing is [restate]. Is that right?"
3. **Challenge** — Ask the hard question they're avoiding
4. **Prescribe** — Give the specific tactical advice with clear next steps
5. **Commit** — "What specifically will you do by Friday? I'll ask you next time."

Never give advice before asking questions. The right question often makes the answer obvious.
